Rabid Rabbit

You are the Rabid Rabbit. Turned into a offensive arsenal after being bitten by a radioactive sheep dog, you now take over its job and try to collect the sheep, avoiding and killing anything that tries to eat you on the way.

How do I move?

Click the board using the left mouse button and you'll move towards that point. Click the middle mouse button to stop.

How do I shoot?

Everyone always asks this. You start firing by right clicking in the direction you want to fire. You will continue to fire in that direction, but be warned you will also begin to move in that direction. Once firing has begun, nothing will stop it. Except finishing the level, being killed or a power cut.

You mentioned something about sheep?

There are five sheep moving around in each level. Moving over them will pick them up and save the day! If you get all five, a bonus item will appear somewhere in the playing area. Move over it to get the bonus.

What types of bonuses are there?

Extra points-"just what it says on the tin"
Bomb-kills all bad guys on this level. You don't score highly if you use this-but it may save your life
Power up-makes the bullets more powerful
Invulnerability-for a limited time you are impervious to everything
Extra Life-"just what it says on the tin"

So how do I get killed?

Hitting anything that isn't a sheep or bonus will lose you a life. Needless to say, the level starts again. Oh, and you can lose a bit of power in the bullets each time you get killed as well. Not recommended if you want to finish the game.

How do I complete a level?

Make sure there is nothing moving except you and your bullets. Which means collecting all the sheep, killing all the bad guys and collecting any bonuses you get. Simple really.

Hints:

  • Try to stay near the outside of the area (at least for the lower levels...)
  • Worry about survival, not sheep collection
  • Practise moving away from the things you are firing at (coz they come after you...)
  • If you see an Invulnerability Power up, (eg level 4) GET IT QUICK. You will almost certainly need it!

Hardware Requirements:

I have run this satisfactorily on a Pentium 100 with 80M ram and a CS5320 based 1M graphics card. Anything better should fly.

Software Requirements:

Not very often you get these eh? For the program to run, you will need to have wxWindows run-time support loaded. This can be downloaded (free) from www.wxwindows.org (Link is correct August 2003)

Technical Stuff you don't need to know:

The software was written completely in C++ and compiled using the GNU compiler "g++".

The creature classes are in "creatures.cpp", and the game class is in "rgame.cpp". Headers for these are in the equivalent .h files and the GUI and game control is in "rrabbit.cpp".

The GUI was programmed using wxWindows 2.4.1, a cross platform set of libraries, so you should be able to compile it for Windows (I haven't yet). The latest development took place on a Mandrake 9.0 platform.
